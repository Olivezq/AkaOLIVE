Clouds, Bullets and Me

Posted on 2022-03-28 In Life Views:
I am just waiting for a person.

WARNING: This is not a very scientific fiction don’t believe anything below.
I shoot a gun to the sky on this big field every day. Even though there’s no athlete or athletic track here. I don’t mean to suicide or kill anyone either. I am just waiting for a person. You can also say, a cloud.
I was a PE teacher. I once was an assassin but that was before a white snake bit me. I bossed athletes around. I shot to the sky to let my students know when to run. Always waiting for them at the end of the track. They were often both tired and happy in the end. And me, just walked around the school or enjoyed the air conditioner down in the school’s secret basement, and that was all. I also liked the scent of grass and books best which reminded me of my youth.
Down in my secret basement, my only companies were books, balls and the gun I used to shoot. I have used that gun for a long time and I will never throw it away. Our headmaster had persuaded me several times to buy a new gun and he even offered me a new office. “Are you farting?”I said. This basement was my palace of sweet memories, only ghosts would give them away.
One day, I peeked out of my basement and surprisingly saw a fluffy cloud. The cloud I saw, was livable. It was lying on the grass, rolling and crawling. It changed from big to small when it sighed. I touched it. Not as foggy as I imagined, but warm and soft. “I’m about to dry. ”it told me. So I took it to my basement and poured water over it.
It quickly expanded and filled the whole room. “Sorry, ”it said, “I was too excited. Let me change.”It shrank and went into my empty bottle as a matter of course, white as milk. “What are you doing here?”I asked. “Oh you wanna know?Okay I’ll tell you.”the cloud said.
It came from the cloud factory. That was a factory on the sky. Its son really loved eating bullets. “Bullets?”I asked. “Yeah,bullets.”it said. So it looked for bullets everywhere in the atmosphere. When people shot, it caught the bullets on the sky. 10 bullets for a spoon, 20 for a plate, 30 for a bowl. It circled around only because its son liked eating bullets.
But later, there was a war. The bullets it caught were decorated with blood. They couldn’t eat hates. After the war, new technology was developed which made guns not popular any more. Still they couldn’t eat much bullets. Finally, it caught one, which was from our school. From then on, it only needed two places to go: our school and its home. “It’s too dangerous.”its son said to it. “How can I not give the best to you?”it replied.
The Sun was too hot today, the water it stored ran out and it fell out of the atmosphere, right landed on our playground. Then it met me.
I put it back into the sky. After that, I shot the sky everyday. It couldn’t take bullets away, because they couldn’t ask for anything from human. That sucks.
Unfortunately, another war began. I hid on my field, retired. Nobody knows where I live. The cloud came down sometimes, to chat with me.
At last, it told me it felt sick.
It never came back.
I shoot a gun to the sky on this big field every day. I am just waiting for a person. You can also say, a cloud. And the day I close my eyes.

A/N: This is a story I post to take part in March writing contest 2020 on Quotev in 2020. It came in third, though it wasn’t a very official contest.
