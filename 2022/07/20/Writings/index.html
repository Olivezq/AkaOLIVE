Dicky looked at two or three pairs of lovers outside the window, with no waves in his heart.
He lowered his head, picked up the ball pen and continued to write Qidong homework.
The green plastic desktop was sprinkled with golden blocks by the sun, dividing his hands and paper. The electric fan whirred and the air conditioner sent out a cool breath.
No one came to pat him on the shoulder and chat with him. He was just an ordinary middle school student, even a little ugly, and his grades had not improved except English and Chinese.
He didn’t care about these feelings. He did his homework everyday and spent a lot of time at home, so he lived a dull life.
Until he met a girl.
Her name was YuChen. She was the first in class 11. Her short hair could not cover her jaw well. Her pink glasses and smiling narrowed eyes made Dicky feel excited, as if she had put on filters, which was so unprecedented.
Clumsy as he was, he did not know how to express his flood of emotions.
He wrote many love letters in English and was teased by his classmates several times. After writing, he had some doubts at first, and then he threw them into the school garbage can one by one. The paper rubbed together, as if he did not know how to place it.
At the end, he slowly became a little confident and gave the first letter.
Only one sentence came back: “What do you like about me? Can’t I change it?”
He was very happy. The note was filled with sentences, “like your student number”, “like your name” and “like your school uniform”···
Finally, “I like everything about you” was in the middle, in big bold, and he described it several times.
His love for her could drown any high-rise building in the world, and the electric wire connected to his heart could keep the electric fan running 24 hours a day. The previously practiced words bloomed in the pen, and the skimming hooks were so astringent.
“Honey,” he wrote in the school blogs, “I heard that you have got chickenpox. Is it serious?”
“It’s wonderful to see her cut her hair (change his line), in the barber shop last week.”
Everyone began to laugh at him, saying that he was infatuated, did not know how to chase people, and chased too closely.
Dicky still wrote Qidong homework every day, but would sigh inexplicably at the little couple when looking out of the window.
One day, he wrote love letters too late, and fell asleep in bed before he had time to do his homework. The next day he did not hand in his homework, and the teacher did not look for him. After going to the bathroom, he walked into the classroom and saw a pile of rolled test sheets and uncorrected exercise books on his seat. He sat in, took out a barely blank paper from it, and began to write love letters again. It seemed that such days had lasted for a long time, and he thought it was good.
His back-desk-mate asked him which university he wanted to go to.
“Tsinghua.” He casually replied which was a habit he had recently formed.
The back-desk-mate showed him a piece of paper, which seemed to be secretly typed. It was full of foreign universities, and he only knew that there was a Harvard in it.
”Which one are you going to go to? “He asked.
”I’m going to MIT. “Said the boy who had been sitting behind him for the whole school year.
“Ah…” He felt MIT very good,” then I’ll go to Stanford University! “ He chose one at random.
They made a deal. In the evening, when Dicky was watching school blogs like he used to when he was bored, he saw his back-desk-mate had changed his avatar, which was the school logo of MIT he saw today. He then changed his original man with a heart into the logo of Stanford. He still remembered that it was a red tree.
He began to study hard. The teachers were all happy and said that he was beginning to do hardwork. The Chinese teacher sat at the table and took a sip of hot tea and said, “This man is trying to force himself. It’s useless for anyone to force him suddenly.”
He got the top 10 in Chinese, the top 20 in mathematics and the top 5 in English. His back table was even harder, who made a sudden progress of 100 forward.
Then, two weeks later, he and three other boys in the class secretly ran out of the classroom in the evening self-study and wandered around the teaching building. He saw YuChen at the back door of class 11. He only felt his heart wrenched and a little hyperemic joy.
“Why don’t you study?” That day, when his front-desk-mate went out to take out the garbage, she saw him leaning up on the railing, half asking and half talking to herself.
“I don’t want to learn.” He looked at her. “It’s boring.”
The front-desk-mate nodded and went back to work to pack her schoolbag.
In class, he sighed again.
“What are you sighing for?! Young people, sighing every day, as if they were war-torn old men. People of your age, it’s not time to sigh!” The Chinese teacher put down the 53 homework in his hand.
“Dicky, really please don’t sigh. You are making the classroom inactive.” The English teacher also stopped turning over the courseware on her hand and seemed to be sighing silently.
The math teacher just smiled with his classmates every time Dicky sighed. She only stared angrily in private and asked him to do his homework well.
He continued his life. Almost every day, he would send sad copies in the school blogs, most of which were written by himself. He also liked to add a rose at the end and adjust the font to a dreamy purple and pink.
He had long stopped chasing YuChen. He tried to chase his front-desk-mate, but then he did not think it was interesting and stopped chasing. He had some good feelings for almost every girl, and had also engaged in online love. Every love was true, but it could never last long.
In class, he would look out of the window. There had been no lovers outside for a long time, but he just liked to look outside, as if he could see the wind and the classroom of class 11 far away.
Looking in the mirror, he would confidently sigh “I’m so handsome”. After the grades came out, he would complain about his waste to his back-desk-mate, and watched the school blogs again in less than three minutes.
He would say hello to the new month, sent encouraging words to motivate himself, and sent lies that he did not believe in love.
Life went on like this. Even on weekends and near the high school entrance examination, he liked to go out with the other three boys and walked on the smoky snack street. He did not like to eat too much, so he would walk for a long time or run at night.
Later, they all graduated, which seemed to be a long time ago, and it seemed to have nothing to do with him.
At this time, Dicky no longer had the green plastic table, nor blue Qidong homework. His past was abandoned in love letters thrown into garbage cans, mixed with his blood, which disturbed his life like clear water. His feathers were plump, hot and sad.
